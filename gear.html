GEAR
When taking pictures, each shot requires different gear; relying on the same formula isn't possible.

The only thing that stays the same is the camera, in my case that's a Canon 5D Mark II, a now 12-year-old camera that's still capable of capturing good images, with the only con being its autofocus limitations.

Lenses are the next important thingâ€”they play a crucial role, determining zoom, background blur (also called bokeh), and more.

For portraits, a 50mm or 85mm lens is the best choice since it mimics natural human vision.

For close-ups like plants, a macro lens excels, allowing you to get really close to the subject without losing the ability to focus.

Completing the holy trinity is a versatile 70-200mm telephoto lens, perfect for distant subjects and nice bokeh effects.
